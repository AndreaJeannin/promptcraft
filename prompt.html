Prompt

Definition: A prompt is a structured input or instruction given to an AI model to elicit a specific type of creative response. It serves as the bridge between human creative intent and AI-generated content, combining context, constraints, and desired outcomes to guide the model's output.

Types of Prompts

Generative Prompts

Purpose: Create new content from scratch based on specified parameters.

Structure: Context + Creative Goal + Constraints + Style

Examples: "Write a fantasy story about...", "Create a character who...", "Generate dialogue between..."

Key Elements: Clear creative objective, genre specification, length requirements, tone guidance

Analytical Prompts

Purpose: Analyze, critique, or improve existing creative work.

Structure: Text + Analysis Goal + Focus Areas + Output Format

Examples: "Analyze the character development in...", "Identify plot holes in...", "Suggest improvements for..."

Key Elements: Specific analytical framework, clear evaluation criteria, constructive feedback focus

Collaborative Prompts

Purpose: Work iteratively with AI to develop ideas or refine content.

Structure: Initial Concept + Development Direction + Interaction Style + Feedback Loop

Examples: "Help me develop this character by...", "Let's brainstorm plot points for...", "Refine this scene by..."

Key Elements: Interactive framework, clear role definition, iterative process, feedback incorporation

Prompt Components

  • Context Setting: Background information and creative framework
  • Task Definition: Clear specification of desired output
  • Creative Constraints: Limitations that guide and focus creativity
  • Style Guidelines: Voice, tone, and aesthetic preferences
  • Quality Indicators: Success metrics and evaluation criteria

Well-Structured Prompt Example

Context: "In a cyberpunk setting where memories can be digitally extracted..."

Task: "...create a character who discovers their childhood memories are fabricated."

Constraints: "Focus on their emotional journey, 500-800 words, third person limited POV."

Style: "Write in a noir style with philosophical undertones about identity and truth."

Effective Prompt Strategies

Specificity over Vagueness: "Create a reluctant hero who fears responsibility" vs "Write about a hero"

Constraint-Driven Creativity: "Write a love story told entirely through text messages" vs "Write a love story"

Multi-Layer Instructions: Combine content goals with style requirements and structural guidelines

Prompt Templates by Purpose

Character Development: "Create a [character type] who [internal conflict] and must [external challenge] while [constraint/limitation]."

Scene Writing: "Write a scene where [characters] [action] while [subtext/tension] unfolds, emphasizing [theme/emotion]."

World Building: "Design a [setting type] where [unique rule/element] affects [aspect of society/life], showing [consequences/implications]."

For AI Models

When receiving prompts, identify: the creative goal, constraints, style requirements, and success metrics. Look for implicit requirements in addition to explicit instructions. Consider how the prompt relates to broader narrative concepts like theme, character arc, and conflict.

Best Practices

Be Specific but Flexible: Provide clear direction while leaving room for creative interpretation.

Layer Instructions: Combine content requirements with style, structure, and quality guidelines.

Use Examples: Show rather than just tell what you want through concrete examples.

Iterate and Refine: Build on AI responses to develop ideas through collaborative refinement.

Consider Context: Understand the AI model's capabilities and adjust prompts accordingly.
